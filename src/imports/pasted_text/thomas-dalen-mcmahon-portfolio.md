PERSONAL PORTFOLIO WEBSITE
FOR: Thomas Dalen McMahon
DEPLOYMENT TARGET: GitHub Pages
STYLE: Minimalistic, credible, personal, polished, not AI-generated

OBJECTIVE
Design a clean personal portfolio website for Thomas Dalen McMahon, a Norwegian informatics student with interests in language technology, sustainability, privacy, data governance, entrepreneurship, and international technology careers. The website should act as a professional but personal home base where visitors can quickly understand who he is, what he is interested in, and explore selected projects, papers, and writing.

The website should feel like it was designed by a thoughtful student/developer, not like a generic AI-generated template. Avoid overly glossy gradients, fake startup language, excessive animations, generic “tech bro” visuals, and clichéd hero sections. The design should be simple, calm, readable, and structured.

CORE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Minimalistic and editorial.
Human, personal, and credible.
Clean typography with strong spacing.
Mostly text-based, but visually balanced.
No heavy animations.
No overly futuristic AI/SaaS aesthetic.
No generic 3D illustrations.
No stock-looking avatars.
No excessive gradients.
No clutter.
Mobile responsive, but desktop should feel especially polished.
The site should look good as a static GitHub Pages portfolio.

VISUAL STYLE
Use a restrained visual identity inspired by personal academic/developer portfolios, independent research blogs, and clean editorial websites.

Suggested aesthetic:

Off-white or very light warm background, not pure sterile white.
Dark charcoal text instead of pure black.
One subtle accent color, such as muted blue, forest green, muted burgundy, or soft graphite.
Thin borders.
Light card backgrounds.
Generous whitespace.
Simple hover states.
Small details that feel hand-designed, such as subtle section labels, quiet dividers, understated project tags, and thoughtful spacing.

Avoid:

Purple/blue AI gradients.
Glassmorphism.
Neon effects.
Floating blobs.
Excessive shadows.
Overly rounded SaaS cards.
Fake AI-generated abstract shapes.
Buzzword-heavy hero copy.
